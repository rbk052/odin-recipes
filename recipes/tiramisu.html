Tiramisu Recipe

Description

Tiramisu is a popular Italian dessert that typically consists of layers of coffee-soaked ladyfingers and a rich and creamy mixture made of mascarpone cheese, eggs, sugar, and sometimes liquor, such as Marsala wine or Kahlua. The dessert is usually dusted with cocoa powder or shaved chocolate on top. Tiramisu has a smooth and velvety texture with a slightly sweet and bitter flavour profile. It is often served chilled and is a favorite dessert among coffee lovers. hahha

Ingredients

  • 400ml Double Cream
  • 250g Mascarpone
  • 75ml Marsala
  • 5 tbsp Golden Caster Sugar
  • 300ml Coffee, made with 2 tbsp coffee granules and 300ml boiling water
  • 175g Sponge Fingers
  • 25g Dark Chocolate
  • 2 tsp Cocoa Powder

Steps

  1. Put the double cream, mascarpone, marsala, and golden caster sugar in a large bowl
  2. Whisk until the cream and mascarpone have completely combined and have the consistency of thickly whipped cream
  3. Pour the coffee into a shallow dish. Dip in a few of the sponge fingers at a time, turning for a few seconds until they are nicely soaked, but not soggy. Layer these in a dish until you've used half the sponge fingers, then spread over half of the creamy mixture.
  4. Using the coarse side of the grater, grate over most of the dark chocolate. The repeat the layers (you should use up all the coffee), finishing with the creamy layer.
  5. Cover and chill for a few hours or overnight. Will keep in the fridge for up to two days.
  6. To server, dust with the cocoa powder and grate over the remainder of the chocolate.
